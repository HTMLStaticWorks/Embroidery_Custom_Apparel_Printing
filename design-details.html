Uniform Guide

Ultimate Guide to B2B Uniform Sourcing & Compliance

Published by Sarah Lin
June 15, 2026
12 Min Read

Planning a comprehensive corporate uniform update requires balancing durability, comfort, cost, and brand visual guidelines. Managing procurement schedules across branches without automated dashboards is usually a manual hassle.

First, consider fabric density. Industrial washing involves hot water loops that cause standard cotton threads to shrink, pulling the embroidered logo boundaries and creating wrinkles. This is why B2B apparel usually relies on custom poly-cotton blends (65% polyester, 35% ringspun cotton) or moisture-wicking synthetic fabrics.

"Always request a digitized stitch proof swatch sample on the actual fabric selection before running the entire production batch. Stitch paths behave differently on mesh polos than on canvas work jackets."

Logo Placements & Digitizing Rules

Standard logo width for left chest positions is 3.5 inches to 4 inches. To keep text readable, letter heights should never fall below 0.25 inches. If your brand vector contains fine elements, DTF transfers are generally recommended over embroidery to avoid thick thread clusters.

The Logistics Checklist

Ensure your custom apparel supplier supports split shipping, allowing division of corporate billing while distributing tracking coordinates to individual local operations managers. This simplifies bulk contract ordering while keeping local teams equipped.
